Requirements Comparison

This table outlines the key differences between functional and non-functional requirements.

Aspect

Functional Requirements

Non-Functional Requirements

Definition

Describe what a system should do

Describe how a system should behave

Focus

Features and functions

Performance, usability, reliability, etc.

Examples

User login, data submission, report generation

System should respond within 2 seconds, support 1000 users

Testability

Can be tested with functional tests (e.g., unit/integration)

Can be tested with performance or usability tests

Importance

Essential for core functionality

Essential for user satisfaction and quality



Artificial Intelligence and Its Importance in Society

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think, learn, and make decisions. AI is transforming various sectors such as healthcare, finance, transportation, and education by enabling automation, improving decision-making, and enhancing productivity. Its growing role in society helps solve complex problems, personalize experiences, and drive innovation. As AI continues to evolve, it holds the potential to greatly improve quality of life and address global challenges.
